Teaching

I teach for the Program for Writing and Rhetoric at the
University of Colorado, Boulder.


Primarily, I focus on genre awareness and media literacy in my first year writing courses at the University of Colorado. I use a lot of creative-writing based pedagogy in the classroom, and we always read a wide range of texts. I love to examine advertising in the classroom to show my students the power of rhetoric in action. Additionally, I'm interested in incorporating technology into my classroom in creative and relevant ways.

In my upper division courses, I strive to make the writing process an active component of curriculum. Therefore, I stress process over product while engaging students in conversations on everything from gender dynamics to infomercials. My favorite classes are those that focus on the intersection of identity politics, rhetoric and media.

For Fall 2015, I'm teaching Television in American Culture. This past summer, I taught both Cross-Cultural Writing and Film, and Gender, Sexuality and New Media.

In October of this year, I presented at the Inaugural Conference for Community Writing. Check out my digital presentation below, "Personifying Chicago."
